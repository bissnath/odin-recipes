Macaroni and Cheese

Descriptions

Macaroni and cheese is the ultimate comfort food, loved for its creamy, cheesy sauce and tender pasta. This classic dish is a favorite for all ages, featuring a rich blend of cheeses that create a velvety, indulgent texture. Whether baked with a golden breadcrumb topping or served straight from the stovetop, it’s a warm and satisfying meal perfect for any occasion.

This recipe takes macaroni and cheese to the next level with a combination of sharp cheddar, creamy gouda, and Parmesan. The cheese sauce is enhanced with a hint of mustard and paprika, adding depth and flavor. Serve it as a hearty main course or a decadent side dish—it’s guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser every time!

Recipe

  • For the Pasta:
    • 1 lb elbow macaroni
    • Salt (for boiling water)
  • For the Cheese Sauce:
    • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
    • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
    • 3 cups whole milk
    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
    • 1 cup shredded gouda cheese
    • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
    • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
    • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Optional Topping:
    • 1 cup breadcrumbs
    • 2 tbsp melted butter
    • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) if baking.
  2. Cook the elbow macaroni in salted boiling water until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, forming a roux.
  4. Gradually pour in the milk and cream, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Simmer until the mixture thickens.
  5. Reduce the heat and stir in the cheddar, gouda, and Parmesan cheeses until melted and smooth.
  6. Add the Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Combine the cooked macaroni with the cheese sauce, mixing well to coat the pasta evenly.
  8. If baking, transfer the macaroni and cheese to a greased baking dish.
  9. For the topping, mix the breadcrumbs with melted butter and Parmesan, then sprinkle over the macaroni.
  10. Bake uncovered for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbling.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy!
