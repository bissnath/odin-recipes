Braised Short Ribs

Descriptions

Braised short ribs are a rich and hearty dish that’s perfect for a cozy dinner or special occasion. Slow-cooked to perfection, these beef short ribs become fall-off-the-bone tender while soaking up the deep, savory flavors of a red wine and herb-infused sauce. The caramelized onions, garlic, and aromatic vegetables create a silky base that enhances the depth of the dish, making every bite indulgent and satisfying.

This recipe is ideal for entertaining or meal prepping, as it tastes even better the next day. Serve the braised short ribs over creamy mashed potatoes, buttery polenta, or alongside crusty bread to soak up the luscious sauce. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just treating yourself, this dish is guaranteed to impress.

Recipe

  • For the Short Ribs:
    • 3 lbs beef short ribs
    • Salt and pepper (to taste)
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • For the Braise:
    • 1 large onion, diced
    • 2 carrots, diced
    • 2 celery stalks, diced
    • 4 garlic cloves, minced
    • 2 tbsp tomato paste
    • 2 cups red wine (e.g., Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot)
    • 2 cups beef stock
    • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
    • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
    • 2 bay leaves

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Season the short ribs generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or oven-safe pot over medium-high heat. Sear the short ribs on all sides until browned, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the ribs and set aside.
  4. Add the onions, carrots, and celery to the pot. Cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the garlic and tomato paste, cooking for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Deglaze the pot with red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Simmer for 5 minutes to reduce slightly.
  7. Return the short ribs to the pot. Add the beef stock, rosemary, thyme, and bay leaves. Ensure the liquid covers the ribs about three-quarters of the way.
  8. Cover the pot with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Braise for 2.5 to 3 hours, checking occasionally to ensure the liquid hasn’t reduced too much (add more stock if needed).
  9. Once the ribs are fork-tender, remove the pot from the oven. Discard the herb sprigs and bay leaves.
  10. Serve the short ribs hot, spooning the sauce over the top. Pair with mashed potatoes, polenta, or your preferred side dish.
